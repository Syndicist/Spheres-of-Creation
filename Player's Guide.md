# The World
The world of this campaign is known as the Great Plane, an expanse that spans the entire universe. Specifically, the campaign takes place on a few of the dozens of continents on the surface of the Great Plane. More specifically, the campaign starts in a small village called Darcey on a northern shore of the continent of Sael. Sael is made up of a number of vassal-countries at the edge of the Infinite Imperium's borders. The Infinite Imperium is an empire spanning many continents of the Great Plane. The village of Darcey is part of the larger vassal-country of Drakeguard, named such for its self-imposed responsibility of guarding against the drakes and dragons of the neighboring northern continent of Dragonheart, an entire continent ruled by dragons. It is unknown why exactly this conflict started, but drakes of Dragonheart make semi-regular raids on the lands of Drakeguard, and the country makes its own excursions to the land of Dragonheart in turn, ensuring the conflict that has gone on for centuries.

# The Party
The party should know eachother well as locals of the village, or at least the surrounding area. Their exact relationships are entirely up to the players themselves. In either case, they should have a reason to specifically want to group up together one way or another.

# Character tips
## Alignment
Characters should be Neutral or Good on the morality alignment scale, but may be Chaotic, Lawful, or Neutral.
## Classes
Generally, classes are unrestricted, however there are certain classes that may feel redundant. Over the course of this campaign, all party members will recieve dragon-themed abilities and obtain dragonic campanions. Classes options that are dragon themed could go well with these abilities, or it may seem too similar. Similarly, Classes with animal companions or other helpful creatures one controls can be overwhelming.
## Languages
Language works somewhat differently in this campaign as opposed to pure Pathfinder, as languages are usually driven by "race". Instead, languages are very much regional. Drakeguard has two official languages: Saelan and High Imperial.
## Species
Referred to as "races" in Pathfinder. There are no hard limits on what species a character is, but players should ask their GM about anything outside of what is core to Pathfinder.
