# The World
The world of this campaign is known as the Great Plane, an expanse that spans the entire universe. The campaign takes place on a few of the dozens of continents on the surface of the Great Plane. More specifically, the campaign starts in a small village called Darcey on a northern shore of the continent of Sael. Sael is made up of a number of vassal-countries at the edge of the Infinite Imperium's borders. The Infinite Imperium is an empire spanning many continents of the Great Plane. The village of Darcey is part of the larger vassal-country of Drakeguard, named such for its self-imposed responsibility of guarding against the drakes and dragons of the neighboring northern continent of Dragonheart, an entire continent ruled by dragons. It is unknown why exactly this conflict started, but drakes of Dragonheart make semi-regular raids on the lands of Drakeguard, and the country makes its own excursions to the land of Dragonheart in turn, ensuring the conflict that has gone on for centuries.

# The Party
The party should know eachother well as locals of the village, or at least the surrounding area. Their exact relationships are entirely up to the players themselves. In either case, they should have a reason to specifically want to group up together one way or another.

# Character tips
## Alignment
Characters should be Neutral or Good on the morality alignment scale, but may be Chaotic, Lawful, or Neutral.

## Classes
Generally, classes are unrestricted, however there are certain classes that may feel redundant. Over the course of this campaign, all party members will recieve dragon-themed abilities and obtain dragonic campanions. Classes options that are dragon themed could go well with these abilities, or it may seem too similar. Similarly, Classes with animal companions or other helpful creatures one controls can be overwhelming.

## Languages
Language works somewhat differently in this campaign as opposed to pure Pathfinder, as languages are usually driven by "race". Instead, languages are very much regional. Drakeguard has two official languages: Saelan and High Imperial.

## Species
Referred to as "races" in Pathfinder. There are no hard limits on what species a character is, but players should ask their GM about anything outside of what is core to Pathfinder.

# Darcey
This campaign starts off in the small coastal town of Darcey. Resting at the mouth of the River Selka, the town is usually peaceful save for its share of the regular Draconic incursion the country of Drakeguard experiences.

## Notable NPCs
### Maris Thorne (Half-orc, Female) – Town Speaker
A pragmatic and sharp-minded leader elected by the townsfolk. Maris prioritizes preparedness over pride, always looking for capable outsiders to reinforce Darcey’s defenses—especially when rumors of draconic movement arise.

### Silae Windbrook (Orc, Female) – Riverwarden
A ranger tasked with patrolling the farmlands and riverbanks. Silae is deeply attuned to the land and has noticed animals behaving erratically—often the first sign of incoming danger. She's looking for a capable group to help her with an investigation.

### Brother Halven (Human, Male) – Priest of Gozreh
Caretaker of a small seaside shrine devoted to Gozreh. Calm and compassionate, he provides healing and guidance—but lately, his prayers have been troubled by visions of fire falling from the sky.

### Takka Reedfoot (Halfling, Female) – Farmer & Militia Captain
A cheerful but fiercely capable halfling who organizes Darcey’s civilian defense. Her farm doubles as a training ground, and she’s known for rallying even the most timid villagers into action.

### Varkesh (Kobold, Male) – Tinkerer
Many raise their brows to Varkesh for his draconic - if diminutive - figure. Nonetheless, he has proven himself an ally to the town with his clever traps and warning systems for the town, though many still distrust him.

## Key Locations
### The Split Dock
Where river and sea traffic meet. A semi-constant bustle of goods, gossip, and sailors. ballistae sit discreetly at its edges, ready to be deployed if airborne threats appear.

### The Salt & Sheaf Inn
The town’s only inn, known for hearty meals and stronger cider. Travelers, merchants, and rarely adventurers pass through regularly, making it a hub for rumors and job opportunities.

### The Grainhold
A fortified granary complex built of thick stone. During attacks, townsfolk retreat here. It contains hidden tunnels leading to the river in case evacuation becomes necessary.

### The Tidemark Shrine
A modest but serene temple overlooking the sea. Offerings for safe voyages and protection from storms are common.

### Windbreak Fields
Farmland stretching inland, dotted with low stone walls that double as defensive lines. Scarecrows are often rigged with bells or mirrors to startle flying predators.

## Points of Interest
### The Northern Wreckline
A stretch of coastline where debris from distant raids washes ashore. Recently, the wreckage has grown more frequent—and stranger, including pieces that seem rusted and gnawed.

### Blackwater Bend
A deep, slow-moving section of the river just upstream. Locals avoid it after dusk. The water is unnaturally dark, and livestock have gone missing nearby.

### The Ashfall Ridge
Low hills north of town where a minor draconic raid was repelled years ago. The earth still bears scorch marks, and strange crystalline growths have begun to emerge.

### The Old Watchtower
A crumbling stone tower once used to spot seaborne threats, but has fallen to ruin after being almost destroyed by one of the draconic incursions long ago.
